## 1. Calorie Calculation

### Important Note
Generic online calorie calculators are notoriously inaccurate. Most underestimate how much you actually burn. Here's the proper calculation:

### Step 1 — BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate)
**Formula (Mifflin-St Jeor for men):**
```
(10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) − (5 × age) + 5
```

**Your calculation:**
```
(10 × 66.5) + (6.25 × 172.7) − (5 × 34) + 5
= 665 + 1079.4 − 170 + 5
= 1,579 kcal/day
```

### Step 2 — Activity Multiplier
- Desk job + ~4,700 steps/day + daily push-ups + 3x/week exercise
- Category: **Lightly Active** (1.375)

```
1,579 × 1.375 = 2,171 kcal/day (TDEE)
```

### Step 3 — Surplus for Muscle Building
Since you're lean and building muscle (not losing fat):
```
2,171 + 300 = ~2,470 kcal/day
```

### Real-World Calibration
Track your food for 2 weeks without changing anything. If your weight stays stable, that's your true maintenance. No calculator beats data from your own body.

## Appendix: Shoulder Exercise Descriptions

### Pendulum Swings
1. Bend forward at waist, support yourself with good arm on table/chair
2. Let affected arm hang straight down, relaxed
3. Make small circles (10 each direction), then front-to-back, side-to-side
4. Keep arm completely relaxed — momentum does the work
5. **Purpose:** Gentle warm-up, creates space in joint

### Wall Slides
1. Stand with back flat against wall
2. Arms at sides, elbows bent 90°, backs of hands touching wall
3. Slowly slide arms up the wall, keeping contact
4. Go only as high as comfortable (stop before pain)
5. Slowly lower back down
6. **Purpose:** Scapular control, safe overhead pattern retraining

### Band Pull-Aparts
1. Hold resistance band at chest height, arms straight in front
2. Pull band apart by squeezing shoulder blades together
3. Keep arms at chest height throughout (not overhead!)
4. Slowly return to start
5. **Purpose:** Rear deltoid + external rotator strengthening

### External Rotation with Band
1. Attach band to door handle or hold in opposite hand
2. Keep elbow tucked at your side, bent 90°
3. Rotate forearm outward against band resistance
4. Keep elbow glued to side throughout
5. Slowly return
6. **Purpose:** Direct rotator cuff (infraspinatus/teres minor) strengthening

### Doorway Chest Stretch
1. Stand in doorway, place forearm on door frame
2. Elbow at shoulder height or slightly below (NOT above shoulder)
3. Step forward gently until stretch felt in chest/front of shoulder
4. Hold 30 seconds, breathe deeply
5. **Purpose:** Opens tight pectorals that contribute to impingement

### Prone Y-T-W Raises
1. Lie face down on bed/floor, arms hanging off edge
2. **Y:** Raise arms at 45° angle (making Y shape), thumbs up
3. **T:** Raise arms straight out to sides (making T shape)
4. **W:** Elbows bent, squeeze shoulder blades (making W shape)
5. Hold each position 2-3 seconds, lower slowly
6. **Purpose:** Lower trapezius + mid-back activation (critical for scapular stability)

### Sleeper Stretch (GENTLE)
1. Lie on affected side, shoulder and elbow both at 90°
2. Use other hand to gently push forearm toward floor
3. **Very gentle pressure** — this should feel like a mild stretch, never pain
4. Hold 30 seconds
5. **Purpose:** Internal rotation mobility
6. **Warning:** Skip this if it causes any pinching or pain
